    Car Detailing 2004 Toyota Echo

    5 minute read

    This is one of the first car detailing jobs I ever did when I started to get more interested in car detailing in 2013/2014.

    I did this as a favour for a friend. I didn’t take too many pictures since the point of this exercise was to get my detailing chops up rather than documenting and making it an expose of my skills.

    Model shown here is a 2004 Toyota Echo Sedan in Indigo Ink Pearl, otherwise known in Japan as the Toyota Vitz/Platz. I found it needed a lot of work but underneath all of that, with some elbow grease and a solid plan of attack you could still bring out what was underneath and get really good results that make it pop out. Not too bad for a 10 year old car that never had a full detailing job done on it before.

    Due to being on a tight budget and not exactly having every tool, and product needed. Or a designated area to do this for that matter, I needed to figure out what was reasonable and within my ability to do in order to clean and protect the car from the elements and to really get it to look good and show it’s true beauty.

    After taking this car to a wand wash to get as much dirt, soil, and other stuff off as I could, I proceeded with an initial walk around and pre inspection. The regimen I was going to go with and plan of attack involved starting off with a claybar immediately after washing and then hitting the car with quick detailer to remove any clay residue and other stuff left behind.

    Although I would have liked to have gone over it using Meguiars #105 to compound and #205 Finishing Polish to correct the paint, at that point in time, I had neither the skill, product, or equipment to do such a correction job.

    Moving on, after finishing up with the claying part of the car, I then used Mothers California Gold Cleaner Wax to clean and smooth out as much of the the minor paint defects as possible. This included swirl marks, light scratches, and “hologramming”.

    Once I buffed all of that out I let it set/cure for about an hour while I grabbed some lunch and a lemonaid (recipe for my awesome lemonaid at the bottom). Just before grabbing lunch I decided to use my Tire Dressing Gel to make the tires pop out and let it set into the rubber so I didn’t get any sling when moving the car later.

    After having lunch, relaxing a bit, and reenergizing I then decided to work on the interior of the car. Here I used Meguiars Interior detailer to get all of the plastics clean and back to the fresh off the lot OEM look. I even got the door jambs and where the trunk meets the interior. Its really the small things that show up after doing a job like this because your eyes tend to get drawn to areas that are different and not consistent just like wiping off spaghetti sauce splatter off the backsplash of your kitchen stove, you end up having the clean everything since it’s one spot that looks out of place and your eyes get drawn to it. Same principle applies here.

    Using a couple tricks I learned from Larry Kosilla of AmmoNYC I got every nook and cranny of the interior plastics with a firm bristled mini paint brush and agitated as much of the dirt out as I could. It looked a hundred times better after that treatment. For the cloth seats although I would have liked to have used an extractor or at the very least a steam machine, again with no access to tools I wasn’t able to do more than a cursory vacuum of the seats and floormats. However I was able to use the interior detailer to clean the pedals and get off most of the dirt giving it a matte off the lot look.

    I then went over the car with a second layer of wax, this time I used Mothers FX Engineered SynWax. Reason being is that synthetic wax lasts longer than a comparable carnauba based wax.

    Products used;

    • Wand wash with foaming soap
    • Meguiars Clay with Griots Speed Shine for lubrication
    • Griots Long Lasting Tire Dressing for that “off the lot OEM” look
    • Meguiars Cleaner wax
    • Mothers FX Engineered SynWax
    • Meguiars Interior detailer (I didn’t take any pics of the interior but it looked a lot better after detailing)
